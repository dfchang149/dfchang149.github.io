Beautiful Design

Of the few major design systems I had learned about, I found myself in awe of Microsoft's new design system, Fluent Design. So much so, that it inspired me to build something that would make use of its sleek and modern design principles.

Thankfully, with all the documentation of the design published by Microsoft, I was able to implement it into the app, in places such as the settings page.

Settings page with lots of customization options

Simplicity

Of the many apps I could have made, I chose tic-tac-toe for its simplicity. It would be a perfect first step into the world of .NET apps and C# applications.

Not only did it simplify the design process, but it gave me a chance to practice making a functional game, with working logic and saving capabilities.

A simple screen to indicate when the player has won

New Environment

Creating Fluent Tic-Tac-Toe allowed me to step into the world of app development and app design, as I learned to create my very first Microsoft Store app.

This meant straying from the comforts of Visual Studio Code as I dove into Visual Studio for the first time, and learned to incorporate the packages and frameworks needed to give the app that Windows 11 feel.

A peek into the inner workings of the app

Dynamic

Although the very concept of the app was seemingly uncomplex, I wanted to give myself a challenge to make the app more interesting.

As a result, I created a system that allowed players to manipulate the board size, dynamically altering the playstyle and difficulty of the game in correspondence.

An unusual 5x5 board size for tic-tac-toe

Adaptive

Although the app itself has only been shown in dark mode, it also has adaptive features that allow the users to change to light theme if they so desired.

In addition, for those who want to sync the app to their system theme, they are able to do so.
